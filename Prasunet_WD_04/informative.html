India is a land of rich cultural heritage and architectural marvels. Here are seven wonders of India that showcase its diversity and historical significance:

Taj Mahal (Uttar Pradesh):
The iconic Taj Mahal is an ivory-white mausoleum built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. It stands as a symbol of eternal love and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Golden Temple(Punjab):
Also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, the Golden Temple is the holiest pilgrimage site for Sikhs worldwide. Designed by the fifth Sikh Guru, Guru Arjan, it houses the Adi Granth (the holy book of Sikhs) and welcomes people of all religions.

Gomateshwara Statue (Karnataka):
The Gomateshwara statue is a 57-foot monolith located on a hilltop in Shravanabelagola. It pays homage to Bahubali, a revered figure in Jainism who attained salvation through meditation. The statue was built around AD 981.

Nalanda University (Bihar):
Nalanda, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was a renowned Buddhist monastery and center of learning from the 7th century BCE to 1200 CE. Scholars from India, China, Korea, Central Asia, and Tibet flocked to Nalanda for knowledge.
